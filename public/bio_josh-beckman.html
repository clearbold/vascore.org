Joshua Beckman, MD

Physician Staff Interpreter

Dr. Joshua A. Beckman joined the staff of Vascore in 2009.  Dr. Joshua Beckman is an Associate Professor of Medicine at Harvard Medical School, and Director of the Cardiovascular Fellowship Program at Brigham and Women’s Hospital, both in Boston, Massachusetts.  Dr. Beckman is actively involved in the practice of vascular medicine, providing direct care to patients, consultant input to other physicians, and participation in the creation of national guidelines for the care of patients with vascular disease. He is the current President of the Society of Vascular Medicine and is active in numerous other professional organizations, including the American College of Cardiology and the American Heart Association. Dr. Beckman is the recipient of the Daniel D. Federman Outstanding Clinical Educator award at Harvard Medical School, the W. Proctor Harvey Young Teacher Award From American College of Cardiology, as well as the inaugural Eugene Braunwald Teaching Award from at Brigham and Women’s Hospital.

Dr. Beckman’s primary research interests lies in understanding the mechanisms of human vascular endothelium function and dysfunction. He has active investigations on the impact of the metabolic disturbances in diabetes, endothelial growth factors, and the impact of chronic inflammatory diseases on human vascular homeostasis. His findings have been published in Circulation, Circulation Research, and JACC, and he has authored or coauthored chapters in more than a dozen textbooks.
