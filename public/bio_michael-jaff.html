In 1997, Dr. Michael R. Jaff founded VasCore, the largest core laboratory of its kind in the United States. Along with being the Medical Director at VasCore, Dr. Jaff is also Medical Director of both the Vascular Center and Vascular Diagnostic Laboratory at the Massachusetts General Hospital, as well as Associate Professor of Medicine at Harvard Medical School. As a clinical consultant, Dr. Jaff is actively involved in all aspects of vascular medicine, including cerebrovascular and peripheral artery disease, venous thromboembolic disease, aneurysmal diseases and all diagnostic strategies in vascular medicine.

Dr. Jaff has published extensively in over 100 peer-reviewed vascular medicine publications. He has had the honor of serving as the President of the Society for Vascular Medicine and Biology as well as being the first person designated as Master of the Society for Vascular Medicine. Dr. Jaff is a Fellow of the American College of Physicians, American College of Cardiology, and American Heart Association, and is a registered physician in vascular interpretation. In 2008, Dr. Jaff received an honorary Doctorate of Science from A.T. Still University School of Medicine, and in 2010 he was the recipient of the “Leaders in Vascular Education” award at the annual Vascular Interventional Advances (VIVA) meeting. Most recently, Dr. Jaff completed his executive Masters in Business Administration from Harvard Business School.
